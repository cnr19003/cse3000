# code_3000
Collection of Assignments for CSE 3000: Contemporary Issues in Computer Science and Engineering

My view on ethics is that it establishes a guide for how individuals and organizations should approach achieving their goals.  It is essential for large organizations to implement a code of ethics so that people within the organization can have a shared understanding of how to conduct their work.  This is especially important for technology companies because technology develops at a rapid pace and a code of ethics can be a guiding principle when innovation brings them beyond the bounds of existing laws.  I also believe that it is important for a code of ethics to be transparent and concrete as to not deceive people about the organization's goals.  A key example of why a code of ethics needs to be transparent and concrete is OpenAI’s contract with the military.  OpenAI has stated on their website that their technology should not be used for mass domestic surveillance or autonomous weapon systems, aligning with Anthropic’s views, however their contract (code of ethics) essentially boils down to “as long as the use of their technology is within the law” (https://openai.com/index/our-agreement-with-the-department-of-war/).  This leaves a loophole that wasn’t allowed with the military’s past deal with Anthropic allowing their “code of ethics” to change at any time as the law can be changed, making it more of an attempt to avoid backlash from the public for compromising their ethics to secure a deal with the military.
